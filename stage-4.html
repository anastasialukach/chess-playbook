Stage 4

Punish Their Mistakes

25 Games · Exploitation

The Mission

You can survive now. Time to win. 36% of your opponents play early queen attacks — and you only beat them 44% of the time. This stage teaches you to punish bad play and convert advantages.

Punishing Early Queen Attacks

When opponent brings queen out early (Qh5, Qf3 before move 5): develop with tempo. Nf6 attacks the queen. Nc6 develops. Every queen move they make = one more piece you develop for free.

The London System

Add this as your second White opening. Against 1...d5 players: d4, Bf4, e3, Nf3, Bd3, O-O. Same 6 moves every game.

Back-Rank Awareness

After castling kingside: if opponent's queen is on the h-file and your h-pawn hasn't moved, check h7 (as Black) or h2 (as White) every single move. If your g-pawn has moved, also check the long diagonal (a1-h8 or a8-h1).

This is the #1 way beginners lose after castling. One unguarded diagonal + an active queen = instant checkmate.

Your Rules

  1. When opponent brings queen out early, develop knights/bishops to attack it.
  2. Try the London System at least 5 times this stage.
  3. After castling, check h2/h7 and long diagonal every move.
  4. Continue all habits from Stages 1-3.

Session Checklist

  • Checked h2/h7 and long diagonal?
  • Maintained all previous stage habits?
