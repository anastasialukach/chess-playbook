Stage 3

Fight for the Center

25 Games · Middle Game

The Mission

Stages 1-2 fixed your openings. Now you need to survive past move 15. This stage teaches CCT scanning and center control — the two skills that catch 90% of mid-game blunders.

CCT Scan

Before EVERY move, ask:

  • Checks — can opponent check me?
  • Captures — can opponent take something?
  • Threats — what's opponent's best move if I do nothing?
If the answer is "checkmate" or "lose my queen" — deal with THAT first.

Center Control

d4-d5-e4-e5 are the four most important squares. A knight on e4 controls 8 squares. A knight on a3 controls 4. Same piece, twice the power.

Development Checkpoint

At move 10, check: Both knights developed? At least one bishop out? Castled? If not — develop before attacking.

Dangerous Patterns to CCT-Scan

Qxh7# Pattern

Bad: Ignore the queen

Castle, Qh5 on board

Ignore Qh5, push e5

Qxh7# — mate

Good: Block with g6

Castle, Qh5 on board

Play g6, block queen

Queen retreats, safe

Your Rules

  1. CCT scan before every move (5 seconds minimum).
  2. Development checkpoint at move 10. Both knights + 1 bishop + castled = ready to attack.
  3. No pawn-grabbing with queen. If winning a pawn pulls your piece from center for 2+ moves, don't take it.
  4. Keep pieces in the center, not on the rim. Knights on c3/f3/d4/e4, not a3/h3.

Session Checklist

  • Did CCT scan before every move?
  • At move 10: both knights + 1 bishop out + castled?
  • Kept pieces in center, not on rim?
  • Reached move 20?
